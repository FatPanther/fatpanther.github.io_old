Attendance, Illness and Make-ups

Because regular attendance is essential to musical growth, a commitment to lessons for the duration of the term is expected when a student enrolls. There can be no tuition deduction or reimbursement for missed lessons. Should a student decide mid-term to discontinue lessons – there will be no tuition reimbursement. Students should arrive no more than 5 minutes early and leave promptly at the conclusion of the lesson. I cannot be responsible for a student once the lesson is over.

Make-up lessons are limited to ONE PER TERM during the make-up lesson week, and only for absences due to illness and with 24 hour notice. Make-up lessons will be given the week following the regular term. There are no exceptions to this policy.

Make up lessons will be offered during the following weeks:

Fall Term: December 15th-19th
Winter Term: March 16th-20th
Spring Term: June 8th-12th

Because schedules are complicated and sports events are sometimes unpredictable, I will issue a copy of my schedule as a swap list. In the case of an anticipated absence, students may arrange to exchange lesson times with another student. Please notify me promptly if a lesson is exchanged or must be cancelled. A free lesson will be offered to students who don’t miss any lessons over the course of a term. This lesson will be given at the usual scheduled lesson time during the week of make-up lessons.

For students with Monday lessons, regular lessons will be taught on holidays. If you are unable to attend, a makeup lesson will be scheduled.

In the event that I must cancel a scheduled lesson, I will provide a make-up or a refund. If lessons must be cancelled due to inclement weather, arrangements will be made for make-up lessons.

Practice

Parents are responsible for making sure that students practice the material they have been assigned a minimum of 5 days a week. Depending on the age of the student, parents will need to sit down with the student each day to ensure that students practice each assignment as laid out on the assignment sheet. This will continue for a few years for younger students. It is essential that parents understand the level of commitment they need to make to ensure each student's success. Progress at the piano depends on daily practice, which should assume the same priority as homework. Practice is most effective when the student is alert and undisturbed. Most students find that setting a regular practice time helps them to be consistent. Some students may choose two short practice sessions rather than one long one. The piano should be in tune and away from family distractions. Fingernails must be short in order to use the hands comfortably and correctly at the keyboard. Together with their parents, students will find a regular time to practice during the day. If it seems difficult in the beginning, just wait! It gets easier! Students will begin to see the results of their steady, daily practice. Parents too will fall into a routine of helping the child complete their assignment.

Students are expected to return all books on loan at the end of the school year, if not before. Students will be charged for any books lost, damaged or not returned.
