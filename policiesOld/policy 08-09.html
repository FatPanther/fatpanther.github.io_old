Attendance, Illness and Make-ups


Because regular attendance is essential to musical growth, a commitment to lessons for the duration of the term is expected when a student enrolls. There can be no tuition deduction or reimbursement for missed lessons. Students should arrive no more than 5 minutes early and leave promptly at the conclusion of the lesson. I cannot be responsible for a student once the lesson is over.


Make-up lessons are limited to ONE PER TERM, and only for absences due to illness and with 24 hour notice. Make-up lessons will be given the week following the regular term during the regularly scheduled lesson time. There are no exceptions to this policy.

Practice


Progress at the piano depends on daily practice, which should assume the same priority as homework. Practice is most effective when the student is alert and undisturbed. Most students find that setting a regular practice time helps them to be consistent. Some students may choose two short practice sessions rather than one long one. The piano should be in tune and away from family distractions. Fingernails must be short in order to use the hands comfortably and correctly at the keyboard. The student and I will decide on a manageable daily practice period that can be accomplished 5 times per week. Together with their parents, students will find a regular time to practice during the day.


Lending Library


The studio is continually building its lending library from which students are able to borrow literature. A record of books on loan will be kept. Students are expected to return all books on loan at the end of the school year, if not before. Students will be charged for any books lost, damaged or not returned.


Purchasing books


Any books purchased by the teacher for the student will be billed in addition to the regular monthly fee. Parents and students should expect to spend a maximum of $25.00 per term, per student on books.

Studio Performances and Group Lessons

Two recitals will be held each year, one in December and one in May. All students will prepare one or two memorized pieces for these events and will be asked to share their music at these recitals.


Group lessons will be held each term, please see the calendar for specific dates.


Family Recital


At the end of winter term students will give a recital at home for their families and friends. Students will create a program of their recital pieces with program notes. The recital will consist of pieces students learn during the fall and winter term and any other pieces that are performance ready. A minimum of three pieces from memory will be performed at this recital.


OMTA Syllabus

Students who have had one full year of lessons and are 8 years or older are required to participate in OMTA syllabus each year.


Other events

Students are required to participate in one other event outside the studio.  This event will be chosen together with the teacher and parents to meet the individual student needs and interests.  Please see attached list of events offered throughout the year.


Materials
All students are required to have an adequate instrument to practice on, a metronome and three ring binder for practice records and assignments.
