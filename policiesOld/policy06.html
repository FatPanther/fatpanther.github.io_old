STUDIO POLICY 2006-2007


For a detailed schedule of this years lessons, recitals, group lessons, and OMTA syllabus, please refer to the calendar page. The studio follows the same schedule as the public school year. The studio is closed during Thanksgiving week, winter break and spring break and no lessons will be taught. Summer lessons will be offered for the first six weeks of summer. Please refer to the calendar for specific dates.

Tuition and Payment

Payment is due at the beginning of each month with the exception of September – payment for September is due the first week of lessons – September 11-15. A free lesson is offered during make-up weeks for students who have not missed any lessons during the term. The last monthly payment for school year lessons is due June 1. There will be no formal billing. However, a late fee of $10.00 will apply to any payments received after the 10th of the month.


The monthly and quarterly tuition rates are based on 33 individual lessons over the 10 month school year. You may pay for more than one month at a time if you prefer.

Attendance, Illness and Make-ups


Because regular attendance is essential to musical growth, a commitment to lessons for the duration of the term is expected when a student enrolls. There can be no tuition deduction or reimbursement for missed lessons. Students should arrive no more than 5 minutes early and leave promptly at the conclusion of the lesson. I cannot be responsible for a student once the lesson is over.


Make-up lessons are limited to ONE PER TERM, and only for absences due to illness and with 24 hour notice. Make-up lessons will be given the week following the regular term or at our mutual convenience. There are no exceptions to this policy.


Make up lessons will be offered during the following weeks:

Fall Term: December 11-15

Winter Term: March 19-23

Spring Term: June 11-15


Because schedules are complicated and sports events are sometimes unpredictable, I will issue a copy of my schedule as a swap list. In the case of an anticipated absence, students may arrange to exchange lesson times with another student. Please notify me promptly if a lesson is exchanged or must be cancelled. A free lesson will be offered to students who don’t miss any lessons over the course of a term. This lesson will be given at the usual scheduled lesson time during the week of make-up lessons.


For students with Monday lessons, there are two holidays during the spring term. The lessons will be rescheduled for a time during the same week as the holiday or during the make-up lesson week.


In the event that I must cancel a scheduled lesson, I will provide a make-up or a refund.


Practice


Progress at the piano depends on daily practice, which should assume the same priority as homework. Practice is most effective when the student is alert and undisturbed. Most students find that setting a regular practice time helps them to be consistent. Some students may choose two short practice sessions rather than one long one. The piano should be in tune and away from family distractions. Fingernails must be short in order to use the hands comfortably and correctly at the keyboard. The student and I will decide on a manageable daily practice period that can be accomplished 5 times per week. Together with their parents, students will find a regular time to practice during the day.


Lending Library


The studio is continually building its lending library from which students are able to borrow literature. A record of books on loan will be kept. Students are expected to return all books on loan at the end of the school year, if not before. Students will be charged for any books lost, damaged or not returned.


Purchasing books


Any books purchased by the teacher for the student will be billed in addition to the regular monthly fee. Parents and students should expect to spend a maximum of $25.00 per term, per student on books.



Performances and Group Lessons


Two recitals will be held each year, one in December and one in May. All students will prepare a piece for these events and will be asked to share their music at these recitals.


Group lessons will be held once per term, please see the calendar for specific dates.


Opportunities to participate in OMTA syllabus, festivals, competitions and other recitals will be made on an individual basis based on the expressed interest of the student and the care taken in preparation for such events.


***NEW!! Family Recital


At the end of winter term students will give a recital at home for their families and friends. Students will create a program of their recital pieces with program notes. The recital will consist of pieces students learn during the fall and winter term and any other pieces that are performance ready. A minimum of three pieces from memory will be performed at this recital.


Materials


All students are required to have an adequate instrument to practice on, a metronome and three ring binder filled with notepaper for practice records and assignments.
